Week 1 – Sprint 1 Contributions

During the first week of my internship, I was introduced to the company, its working environment, and organizational culture. I was given a tour of the workplace and briefed on the company’s work ethics, expectations, and operational guidelines. This helped me better understand how teams collaborate and maintain professionalism in a real-world working environment.

Following the introduction, I spent time getting familiar with my colleagues through communication and interaction. This allowed me to build initial connections and become more comfortable working within a team setting. Later on the first day, we were assigned a group project and discussed how to distribute responsibilities effectively. The tasks assigned were intentionally challenging, aiming to assess our problem-solving abilities, adaptability, and technical interests.

My teammate and I chose to work on a voice cloning AI project, which was one of the more complex options available. Despite its difficulty, I found it highly engaging and was motivated to take on the challenge. We began by conducting thorough research to understand the fundamentals of voice cloning, artificial intelligence models, and the technical requirements needed to build a working prototype. In addition to this, I created structured research notes to better understand the context of voice cloning, including how different models operate, the differences between few-shot and zero-shot approaches, and the overall system pipeline. This ensured that our approach was grounded in understanding rather than blindly implementing features, allowing us to make more informed and technically sound decisions throughout the project.

We adopted a Scrum-based workflow, organizing our work into sprints and assigning tasks individually to ensure steady progress. During Sprint 1, we evaluated different AI models and decided to use GPT-SoVITS, as it provided a strong balance between performance, flexibility, and cost. We first tested its WebUI to assess its reliability before integrating it into our system.

Due to constraints such as limited resources, time, and expertise, we chose to leverage open-source models instead of building an AI system from scratch, all this approaches and conclusions was documented, which was better refined on week 2. However, integrating GPT-SoVITS presented several technical challenges, particularly in environment setup and system compatibility.

A major portion of my contribution in Week 1 was focused on deep technical implementation, particularly in setting up, integrating, and stabilizing the GPT-SoVITS system. My teammate and I initially worked on different versions due to incompatibilities in Python versions, dependencies, and system configurations. As a result, we each had separate working environments and even separate versions of the website that only worked with our own setups, which created inconsistencies and made collaboration difficult.

We encountered numerous issues such as dependency conflicts, missing libraries, incorrect configurations, and failures when running training or inference. Through repeated debugging, environment reconfiguration, and testing, I played a key role in identifying compatibility issues and standardizing the system to a stable version that worked across both setups. This allowed us to unify our development environment and collaborate effectively using GitHub.

Beyond environment setup, I was heavily involved in integrating our website with the GPT-SoVITS backend. This involved connecting endpoints for both training and inference, ensuring that user actions on the frontend correctly triggered backend processes. However, this was not just simple API integration. A large part of my contribution involved preparing and handling data using Python, which was initially an unfamiliar area for me. Despite this, I made a strong effort to learn and understand how Python is used in audio processing and AI pipelines, rather than simply following existing implementations.

Through this process, I worked on developing preprocessing scripts that handle critical input preparation before data is passed into the model. This included slicing long audio into smaller, manageable segments, applying denoising techniques to improve audio clarity, and ensuring that all input data followed the correct format required by GPT-SoVITS. I also implemented validation steps to check for consistency in audio length, sampling rate, and transcript alignment, as these directly impact training quality and model performance.

These steps were essential because the quality of input data directly impacts pronunciation accuracy, tone consistency, and overall output quality.I also contributed to building and refining the full training and inference pipelines, ensuring smooth data flow from user input to generated output. During this process, I encountered and resolved multiple issues such as failed inference requests, unstable outputs for long text, incorrect pronunciation, and inconsistent tone and pauses. These required continuous debugging, iterative testing, and refinement of both backend workflows and model interactions.

We developed a web-based prototype that allows users to upload voice recordings for training, trigger model training, select trained models, upload reference audio for tone guidance, provide transcripts, and generate cloned speech from text. The system was designed with a real-world use case in mind, specifically enabling lecturers’ voices to be cloned for educational content.

Testing was a major part of my contribution. I conducted experiments using different audio durations to evaluate how training data length affects both accuracy and speed. I observed that shorter audio resulted in faster training but lower accuracy, while longer audio improved voice similarity but increased processing time. I also tested generated outputs for pronunciation, tone, pauses, and punctuation handling to ensure consistent quality.

To improve system performance, I implemented optimization techniques such as text chunking for long inputs, retry mechanisms for failed outputs, and audio stitching to produce smoother playback. These optimizations significantly improved system stability and usability.

In addition, I explored alternative implementations such as integrating paid APIs like ElevenLabs by building a separate prototype. Through this, I concluded that although such APIs provide high-quality output, they are not suitable for scalable deployment due to cost constraints.

By the end of Week 1, I had successfully contributed to unifying our environments, integrating the website with GPT-SoVITS, and ensuring that both training and inference pipelines were functioning reliably through extensive debugging, testing, and optimization.

Week 2 – Sprint 2 Contributions

During the second week, my contributions became more advanced and focused on deeper system-level understanding, documentation, architectural design, and deployment planning, while continuing technical validation and refinement of the system. Compared to Week 1, where the focus was on implementation and debugging, Week 2 required me to consolidate the entire system into a structured, well-explained, and production-ready design.

A major part of my work involved creating comprehensive technical documentation that not only described what we built, but clearly explained how and why each component works. I documented the full system workflow from end to end, covering how input data is processed, how GPT-SoVITS performs training and inference, and how outputs are generated and returned to the user. This required a strong understanding of the internal pipeline, including preprocessing, feature extraction, semantic modeling, and audio synthesis.

I also designed detailed system architecture diagrams using draw.io, carefully mapping out all components including the frontend, backend server, audio processing pipeline, and GPT-SoVITS inference server. I ensured that the diagrams clearly showed data flow, API interactions, and system boundaries, making the architecture easy to understand even for non-technical stakeholders. This was not just diagram creation, but required me to deeply understand and translate a complex system into a clear visual representation.

In addition, I documented key system features, technical design decisions, model limitations, and important ethical and legal considerations related to voice cloning. This included explaining risks such as misuse of cloned voices and the importance of consent, which added a critical real-world perspective to the project.

Another major contribution in Week 2 was my in-depth research and comparative analysis of different text-to-speech models. I independently created two detailed documents comparing few-shot and zero-shot models, including performance metrics such as accuracy, speed, and training time, as well as feasibility, scalability, and real-world usability. These were not just theoretical comparisons, but were supported by hands-on testing and observations.

From this research, I justified the selection of GPT-SoVITS as the best few-shot model due to its high voice similarity and flexibility, and CosyVoice as the best zero-shot model due to its speed and ease of use. I also personally set up and tested CosyVoice, configuring its environment and running experiments to evaluate its performance. Through this, I concluded that while zero-shot models are faster, they are less stable and less accurate for long-form speech, making them less suitable for our use case.

In addition to documentation and research, I also made a strong effort to deeply understand cloud development and deployment strategies. I explored how to deploy our website effectively and efficiently, including how to structure services across cloud infrastructure, manage GPU-based workloads, and ensure scalability. I studied deployment concepts such as separating frontend and backend services, running model inference on dedicated servers, and using containerization tools like Docker alongside orchestration tools like Kubernetes.

A key technical contribution in Week 2 was my work on cloud deployment planning. I developed a detailed deployment strategy that outlined how the system can be scaled and hosted in a production environment. This included designing a cloud architecture that separates the frontend, backend, and model servers, identifying GPU requirements for model inference, and proposing the use of containerization tools such as Docker and orchestration tools like Kubernetes. I also considered cost efficiency, scalability, and system reliability, ensuring that the design is practical for real-world deployment.

Throughout Week 2, I continued performing technical validation and testing to ensure system stability and performance under different conditions. I refined workflows, validated outputs, and ensured that the system could handle realistic usage scenarios. Compared to Week 1, my role evolved from mainly implementing and debugging to taking ownership of system understanding, design, and justification, which required a deeper level of technical thinking and responsibility.

Through this experience, I gained strong hands-on exposure to integrating AI models into real-world systems and ensuring they function reliably end-to-end. This helped me understand how to design workflows that are not only functional but also robust and scalable. I also improved my ability to optimize systems using techniques such as chunking, retry mechanisms, and audio stitching, and understood how these optimizations directly impact user experience, stability, and performance in real-world applications.

I became more confident in making technical decisions by balancing these trade-offs, rather than focusing purely on model performance. I also strengthened my problem-solving skills through continuous debugging and system refinement, learning how to approach issues systematically by isolating problems, testing hypotheses, and iterating on solutions.

Beyond technical skills, I found it particularly interesting and rewarding to learn concepts outside of traditional IT, such as how human speech, tone, and pronunciation are modeled by AI systems. Overall, this internship has significantly improved my technical abilities and strengthened my confidence in working with AI systems. More importantly, it has helped me develop a structured, research-driven approach to solving unfamiliar problems, the ability to adapt quickly to new technologies, and the confidence to design and implement practical, real-world solutions from the ground up.
